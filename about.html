Since childhood, my practice has been rooted in studio art, but in recent years I have expanded into photography and filmmaking as I continue to explore new ways of telling stories.

My work is deeply rooted in family, small everyday moments, and a sense of nostalgia, for both real memories and imagined ones that feel just as vivid. I’ve always been drawn to the lives of others, often finding myself seeing the world through their experiences. Their stories have become central to my work and a constant source of inspiration, becoming the foundation of my work.

I create through both documentation and intentional, theme-driven projects, often focusing on family, womanhood, and strength. I’m interested in how these ideas exist beyond stereotypes, showcasing how strength can be quiet, and how identity can exist outside of societal expectations.

At its core, my work is about holding onto what might otherwise be lost, preserving moments, honoring stories, and creating space for voices to be seen and felt.
